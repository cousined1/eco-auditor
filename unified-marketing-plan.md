## POSITIONING

**Core narrative:** Not "ESG transformation" — it's **cost avoidance, contract readiness, and audit defensibility for SMBs**.

**Central promise:** Eco-Auditor makes SMBs customer-ready and audit-ready for carbon reporting — without enterprise software complexity.

**Supporting line:** "Cheaper than a single consultant engagement, but built for real review and signoff."

This framing works because SMBs don't buy "sustainability" — they buy "my customer is making me fill out this form" and "I need to answer procurement requests without hiring a consultant."

### Three Message Pillars (Use Everywhere)

| Pillar | Message | Buyer Trigger |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Cost Avoidance** | "Avoid consultant spend, spreadsheet rework, and late-stage scramble." | Budget-conscious CFO |
| **Contract Readiness** | "Answer customer and procurement requests fast." | Lost a deal over compliance |
| **Audit Defensibility** | "Every estimate, assumption, and report is traceable." | Facing audit or review |

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary
- CFOs and controllers at $10M–$500M companies
- Ops leaders at multi-site manufacturing or distribution businesses
- Sustainability leads at exporters and supplier-heavy SMBs

### Priority Verticals (Start Here)
1. **Food & Beverage** — Facility energy, freight, packaging, supplier data = obvious pain
2. **Light Manufacturing** — Multi-site, export pressure, CBAM exposure

### Why These First
These buyers already deal with the data. The pain is real and immediate. ROI is easy to demonstrate.
